# Craft

Nora is not trying to become the smartest study app.

It is trying to become the most thoughtful place to learn.

Most educational software measures productivity. Nora wants to cultivate understanding.

Every feature we add should make learning feel a little calmer, a little more human, and a little more memorable.

This document is not a roadmap. It is a collection of design beliefs that guide Nora's future.

---

## The Shift

Version 1 focused on capability.

Can we build evidence-based learning? Can we build a knowledge graph? Can we build an AI tutor?

Version 2 is about craftsmanship.

Not more features. Better feelings.

The goal is no longer to ask:

> "What should Nora do?"

Instead we ask:

> "How should learning feel?"

---

## Memory, not Personalization

Many applications personalize. Few applications remember.

Personalization is:

> "You like biology."

Memory is:

> "I remember this idea was difficult for us last Thursday."

The future of Nora is built around memory. The application should remember before it asks.

---

## Seven Principles of Craft

### 1. Memory, not personalization

The application remembers your journey. Not your preferences — your struggles, your breakthroughs, your growth over time.

### 2. Places, not pages

Every screen feels like somewhere you can return to. Today. The Garden. The Journal. The Study Corner. Each has its own mood, its own pace.

### 3. Ritual, not engagement

Learning becomes part of daily life — not another productivity metric. No urgency. No guilt. Just a quiet rhythm you look forward to.

### 4. Atmosphere changes with time

Morning feels different from evening. Nothing changes mechanically. Only emotionally. The software acknowledges that you exist in time.

### 5. Silence is part of the experience

Not every success needs confetti. Sometimes a single flower blooming is enough. Restraint is confidence.

### 6. Slow interactions feel intentional

A 250ms pause before the companion speaks. A gentle fade instead of an instant swap. Speed communicates urgency. Slowness communicates care.

### 7. Imperfection makes things feel alive

The companion blinks at irregular intervals. Leaves don't all sway together. Clouds move at different speeds. The brain notices when things aren't synchronized — and reads it as life.

---

## What Nora Will Never Become

Nora will never use guilt as motivation.

Nora will never hide core learning behind manipulation.

Nora will never replace understanding with generated answers.

Nora will never optimize for addiction over education.

Nora will never punish people for taking a break.

Nora will never sound like every other AI assistant.

---

## Three Futures

One day, Nora may notice the changing seasons.

One day, the companion may remember your anniversary of joining.

One day, finishing a review may feel like closing a favorite book.

One day, opening Nora at night may feel genuinely different from opening it in the morning.

These aren't promises. They're directions.

---

## A Question Before Every Pull Request

Before merging a new feature, ask:

- Does this make Nora feel more alive?
- Does this help someone understand more deeply?
- Would this still belong here five years from now?

If the answer is no, don't build it yet.

---

## The Design Language

Five words that describe how Nora should feel:

**Gentle.** Never loud.

**Patient.** Never urgent.

**Honest.** If we're not sure, we say so.

**Quiet.** Not every moment needs words.

**Handmade.** Not generated.

---

Knowledge grows slowly.

The software should grow the same way.
