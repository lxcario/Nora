# Design Principles

Five rules that guide every decision in Nora.

---

## 1. AI should teach. Not replace thinking.

The AI asks questions. It finds gaps. It helps you research.

It never writes your explanation. It never does the recalling for you. If you didn't think it through yourself, you didn't learn it.

---

## 2. Growth over streaks.

We don't count consecutive days. We count understanding.

A student who skipped three days but came back and finally understood recursion is doing better than one who reviewed 50 easy cards on autopilot for a streak badge.

Missed days become help quests for friends, not punishments.

---

## 3. Evidence before confidence.

Every claim needs a source. Every evaluation is grounded. When Nora isn't sure, it says so.

"Unverified" is always labeled. Fabricated citations don't exist. If fewer than two sources are found, the system admits it rather than filling the gap with hallucination.

---

## 4. Learning should feel safe.

No public leaderboards. No shame for wrong answers. No anxiety timers on core study flows.

The pet gets sad but recovers. The garden wilts but regrows. Mistakes are labeled "still growing" — because they are.

---

## 5. Small moments matter.

A one-line companion message. A flower instead of a trophy. "One more memory" before the last card.

These aren't features. They're the feeling that makes someone open the app again tomorrow.

---

## 6. Every interaction should leave the learner a little calmer than before.

This doesn't mean every screen is quiet or passive. It means that even when Nora points out a mistake, asks a difficult question, or encourages another review — the learner should leave feeling supported rather than pressured.

Nora is a place people want to return to. Not just a tool they use.

---

*Every new feature must pass one question: "Does this make studying feel more human?" If the answer is no, don't build it.*
